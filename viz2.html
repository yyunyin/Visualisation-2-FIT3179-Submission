Behind the leaf, the trend and the prep

Matcha didn't appear out of nowhere. It began as a place-bound ritual, evolved through different country practices, and only recently broke into the mainstream.

This visualisation maps that journey — where it's produced, how interest surged from 2020-2024, and how preparation choices turn a simple tea into anything from a light sip to a dessert-like drink.

Prooducers & Roots

Who produces matcha today, when did matcha gain traction?

Over half of the world's matcha comes from Japan. China and South Korea follow, supplying much of the export volume. The rest of this infographic focuses on these three key producers, as they shape both the supply and global perception of matcha.

From Tang-era powdered tea to today's viral lattes, matcha's story is one of constant reinvention. Its modern boom transformed it from a ceremonial tradition into a global trend — driven by the innovation and exports of East Asia's leading producers.

The Chemistry & Composition

How does the origin of matcha affect its chemical composition? Measured per 100mg.

Matcha also appeals with its healthy profile. Packed with polyphenols and flavonoids (antioxidant and anti-inflammatory support), L-theanine + caffeine for calm, steady focus, plus vitamin C and chlorophyll that contribute to immunity and that vivid green.

The proportions of these compounds vary by origin—cultivar, shading, and milling practices in Japan, China, and South Korea produce slightly different nutrient compositions as shown in the radars.

Matcha vs. Coffee vs. Tea

How the three compare across key metrics?

To see where matcha fits in the daily drink spectrum, it's compared with coffee and green tea — its two closest functional counterparts. Coffee sets the benchmark for caffeine and energy, while green tea highlights how processing and preparation shape matcha's unique nutritional makeup.

Coffee tops caffeine, but matcha delivers a much larger total nutrient load due to consuming the whole leaf. Green tea lands in the middle: gentler caffeine and fewer antioxidants than matcha. Net: matcha pairs steadier energy with the richest antioxidant profile.
